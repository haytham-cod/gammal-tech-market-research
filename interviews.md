User Interviews – Preventive Health Habits

Overview
To better understand how people manage their health and whether they would adopt preventive health technology, three informal interviews were conducted. Participants were asked about their health habits and their interest in an app that tracks meals and warns users about potential health risks early.

Interview 1:

Participant: Ahmed, 27
Occupation: Software Engineer
Interview Type: Text chat

Health Habits

Ahmed said he does not actively track his health or diet.

"Honestly I only think about my health when I get sick."

He mentioned that he sometimes tries to eat healthier but does not follow any structured plan.

Preventive Behavior

Ahmed does not currently use any health or fitness apps.

"I downloaded a calorie app once but stopped using it after a few days."

Reaction to the Idea

Ahmed said he would be interested in an app that automatically detects unhealthy habits.

"If an app could warn me early like 'you're eating too much sugar this week', that would actually be useful."

Key Insight

Young professionals may want preventive health tools but prefer automation rather than manual tracking.

Interview 2:

Participant: Salma, 34
Occupation: Marketing Manager
Interview Type: Voice chat

Health Habits

Salma tries to maintain a balanced diet and occasionally exercises.

"I try to eat healthy but when work gets busy I end up eating fast food."

She sometimes tracks calories but finds it tedious.

Preventive Behavior

She does basic preventive actions like annual checkups.

"I only track things seriously when my doctor tells me to."

Reaction to the Idea

Salma liked the idea of a meal-tracking app that warns users early about unhealthy habits.

"If it could connect meals with health risks, that would make me pay attention."

Key Insight

Users are interested in health insights, not just data logging.

Interview 3:

Participant: Mahmoud, 45
Occupation: Taxi Driver
Interview Type: Text chat

Health Habits

Mahmoud said he rarely tracks his health.

"I don't really check anything unless I feel pain."

He mentioned that long working hours make healthy habits difficult.

Preventive Behavior

He only visits doctors when necessary.

"Doctors are expensive so I avoid going unless something is wrong."

Reaction to the Idea

Mahmoud said an app that gives early warnings might help.

"If it tells me early that something might become a problem, I would try it."

Key Insight

Preventive health apps may be valuable for people who avoid frequent doctor visits.

Overall Findings

Three patterns emerged from the interviews:

1. Preventive health habits are limited

Most participants only think about their health after symptoms appear.

2. Manual tracking is unpopular

Participants do not like logging meals or data manually.

3. Early warnings are attractive

All participants expressed interest in an app that automatically detects unhealthy patterns and provides early warnings.

Opportunity for healthcare.gammal.tech

A preventive health platform could stand out by focusing on:

Automatic meal and habit tracking

Early risk detection

Simple insights rather than complex data dashboards

This could help users shift from reactive healthcare to preventive healthcare.
